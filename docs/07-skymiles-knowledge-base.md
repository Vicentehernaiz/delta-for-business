## 1. What are SkyMiles

SkyMiles are Delta's travel currency — earned every time you fly, spend, or engage with Delta's partner network, and redeemable for flights, upgrades, and experiences across more than 30 airlines worldwide.

Every dollar spent on an eligible Delta fare earns miles. Every purchase on a Delta Amex card earns miles. Every stay at a partner hotel, every car rental, every rideshare earns miles. Miles don't expire as long as your account stays active, and they never lose their core value: getting you and your team further for less.

**For the business context:** SkyMiles operate on two levels simultaneously — as a personal currency for the individual traveler, and as a company asset through SkyMiles for Business. Understanding this duality is the key to understanding the entire Delta for Business ecosystem.

## 2. Two mile accounts, one flight — the dual-earn mechanic

When your company enrolls in SkyMiles for Business, every eligible flight creates miles in **two places at once** — your company account and your employee's personal account — with no reduction to either.

**Company account:** Earns miles based on the base fare, building a balance your business controls directly. Rates depend on route type and cabin class (up to 10 miles per dollar on premium non-hub routes). The company decides how and when to use these miles.

**Employee personal account:** Earns their own personal SkyMiles and Medallion Qualifying Dollars on the exact same ticket, advancing their individual status just as they would without any company program in place. Their Medallion multiplier applies normally. Their MQDs count toward their personal tier progression.

Two separate accounts. Two separate balances. One flight pays both.

**Ownership rule:** Company miles are owned and managed by the business. Employee miles are personal and always belong to the individual traveler. The company cannot access, revoke, or redirect an employee's personal miles.

## 3. What miles unlock — company vs personal

### Company miles (for the business)

The company's SkyMiles for Business account can use earned miles in exactly four ways:

**Travel credits (eCredits)**
Convert company miles into Delta eCredits and apply them to future bookings. Value: approximately 1.5 to 2 cents per mile. Processed in under 4 hours. Since June 2025, also redeemable via GDS and travel agencies. Limitation: one eCredit per ticket.

**Transfer miles to employees**
Transfer company miles directly into any employee's personal SkyMiles account. The employee spends them however they want — flights, upgrades, experiences. Transfer is immediate, unidirectional, and irreversible. This is the most commonly used redemption option.

**Sky Club access**
Use company miles to purchase an individual Delta Sky Club membership for a traveler on your team. Covers 12 months of lounge access.

**Accelerate employee status**
Redeem company miles for a Silver Medallion certificate and give a team member 12 months of elite benefits starting immediately. Processed in one business day. Especially valuable for employees close to but not quite at Silver threshold on their own.

### Personal miles (for the employee)

The employee's personal SkyMiles can be used for:

**Award flights**
Book flights on Delta and 30+ partner airlines worldwide using personal SkyMiles. No blackout dates on Delta-operated flights.

**Cabin upgrades**
Use miles to upgrade from Main Cabin to First Class or Delta One on eligible flights.

**Companion certificates**
Platinum and Reserve Amex cardholders receive an annual companion certificate — one ticket in Main Cabin or First Class for the cost of taxes and fees only.

**Delta Sky Club day passes**
Purchase lounge access with miles on the day of travel.

## 6. Key rules and edge cases

### Miles don't expire

SkyMiles never expire as long as the account remains active. There is no explicit activity requirement, but Delta reserves the right to close accounts with extended inactivity.

### Basic Economy exclusion

Basic Economy tickets do not earn company miles through SkyMiles for Business. They may earn limited personal miles for the employee depending on fare rules.

### Hub penalty

Flights through Delta's major hubs (ATL, DTW, MSP, SLC) earn company miles at approximately half the rate of non-hub routes. This is a significant factor for companies based in hub cities. Personal miles for the employee are not affected by this hub penalty.

### One eCredit per ticket

When the company redeems miles as eCredits, only one eCredit can be applied per ticket. Companies with large mile balances should plan redemptions accordingly.

### Eligible flight revenue definition

The program year runs February 1 through January 31. "Eligible flight revenue" means the base fare excluding taxes and government-imposed fees. Only tickets on Delta-marketed and -operated flights count.

### eCredit mechanics (detailed)

When a company converts miles to eCredits via the management portal, the credits are generated within 4 hours at approximately 1.5–2 cents per mile. eCredits function as Delta travel credits applied at checkout to reduce fare cost. Since June 2025, they are redeemable through GDS and travel agencies (Concur, TMC workflows), not just delta.com. eCredits typically expire 1 year from issuance. The conversion is irreversible — miles cannot be restored once converted. Cannot be used for Basic Economy. Cannot be stacked — one eCredit per ticket maximum.

## 7. Double-earn activation — how it actually works

The double-earn is **not fully automatic** just because both accounts exist. Here is the exact activation flow:

1. **Company enrolls** in SkyMiles for Business (free, instant)
2. **Employee links** their personal SkyMiles ID to the company's Business ID through the management portal (one-time manual step)
3. **Business ID auto-applies** to every eligible booking the linked employee makes from that point forward
4. **Both accounts earn** on every flight — no per-trip action required

The one manual step is the employee linking their SkyMiles account (#2). Once done, everything is automatic. The employee doesn't enter anything at checkout. The company doesn't approve individual flights. The system handles dual crediting silently.

**If the employee doesn't link:** The company doesn't earn on that employee's flights, even if the company has a SkyMiles for Business account. The employee still earns their own personal miles as usual.

## 8. SkyMiles vs Medallion — the critical distinction

These are the two most commonly confused concepts in the Delta ecosystem, and the web must make the distinction unmistakable.

**SkyMiles = currency (your balance)**
- Earned from flights, card spend, partners
- Spent on award flights, upgrades, Sky Club passes
- Balance goes up when you earn, down when you spend
- Never expire (if account active)
- Both the company and the employee earn SkyMiles (in separate accounts)

**Medallion = status (your tier)**
- Earned by accumulating MQDs (Medallion Qualifying Dollars) within a calendar year
- Determines how Delta treats you: boarding zone, upgrade eligibility, lounge access, bonus earning rate
- Four tiers: Silver ($5K) → Gold ($10K) → Platinum ($15K) → Diamond ($28K)
- Qualification is automatic — no application needed
- Status lasts the rest of the qualifying year + the entire next year
- **Strictly personal** — belongs to the individual, not the company

**How they interact:** Flying earns both. A $1,000 flight earns SkyMiles (added to your balance) AND MQDs (counted toward your Medallion tier). But you can also earn SkyMiles without earning MQDs (partner hotels, shopping, etc.) and MQDs without spending SkyMiles (card Headstart). They are tracked independently.

**In the business context:** The company's SkyMiles for Business account earns company SkyMiles (currency). The employee's personal account earns both personal SkyMiles (currency) and MQDs (toward their personal Medallion status). Medallion qualification is entirely automatic — when MQDs cross a threshold, status activates immediately. No application, no enrollment, no company involvement.

### How Medallion benefits the company (indirectly)

The company cannot earn or hold Medallion status. But employees with Medallion status provide indirect value:
- Better flight experience → more productive travel
- Priority rebooking during disruptions → fewer missed meetings
- Complimentary upgrades → cost savings on premium cabin purchases
- Corporate Priority stacks with Medallion: a Gold Medallion employee on a corporate ticket outranks a Gold Medallion non-corporate traveler in the upgrade queue

The company can also **grant Silver Medallion certificates** from its company miles balance, giving employees 12 months of Silver status immediately.
