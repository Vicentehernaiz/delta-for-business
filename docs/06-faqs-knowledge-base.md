## A. Program structure — "Which program is right for me?"

**Pages:** `/programs/`, `/resources/faq/`, homepage Program Finder

### Q: What are the four Delta for Business programs?

Delta offers four programs based on company size and travel spend. **Individual Business Traveler** is for solo travelers who want personal perks without employer involvement — free, no flight benefits, partner discounts only. **SkyMiles for Business** (Small Business) is for companies with 1–50 travelers spending $5K+/year — the company and employees both earn miles on every flight. **Mid-Market (MSA)** is for companies with 50–500 travelers — adds negotiated fares, Corporate Priority, and a dedicated account manager. **Enterprise (CSA)** is for 500+ travelers — full global coverage, unused ticket transfer, and 24/7 Corporate Solutions.

### Q: Which program is best for my company?

Take the 60-second quiz at `/tools/program-selector/` — it matches your company size, travel spend, and goals to the right program. Or use this shortcut: if it's just you, start with Individual. If your company spends $5K–$50K on Delta flights per year, SkyMiles for Business. If $50K–$300K, contact sales for a Mid-Market agreement. If $300K+, contact sales for Enterprise.

### Q: Can I switch programs later?

You don't need to switch — SkyMiles for Business tiers (Member → Plus → Elite) upgrade automatically when you cross the spend threshold. If your company grows into the Mid-Market or Enterprise range, a Delta sales representative will reach out, or you can contact sales proactively. There's no penalty or restart.

### Q: Is there a cost to join any program?

Individual Business Traveler and SkyMiles for Business are both free to join with no enrollment fee. Mid-Market and Enterprise agreements are negotiated with Delta Corporate Sales — no enrollment fee, but terms are customized.

## B. SkyMiles for Business — earning and mechanics

**Pages:** `/programs/small-business/`, `/skymiles/how-it-works/`, `/skymiles/earning/`, `/skymiles/double-earn/`, `/resources/faq/`

### Q: Do employees also earn personal miles when the company earns?

Yes — this is the core mechanic. When an employee flies with the company's Business ID attached, two things happen simultaneously: the company account earns miles based on the fare, and the employee's personal SkyMiles account earns their own miles and MQDs at their normal rate. Neither account reduces the other. This is mechanically real, not a marketing approximation.

### Q: What's the minimum spend to start earning company miles?

Joining is free with no minimum. But the company only starts earning miles once it reaches the **Plus tier**: 5 registered unique travelers and $5,000 in eligible flight revenue per program year (February 1 through January 31). Eligible revenue = base fare excluding taxes and government fees. Basic Economy does not count.

### Q: What do Member, Plus, and Elite mean?

These are the company's program tiers within SkyMiles for Business — completely separate from Medallion status (which is the employee's personal status). **Member** is where every company starts — free, portal access, personalized offers, but no mile earning. **Plus** activates at 5 travelers + $5K/year — the company starts earning real miles at up to 10 miles per dollar. **Elite** activates at 5 travelers + $300K/year — adds a 15% bonus on all miles earned plus a dedicated account manager. The upgrade is automatic when thresholds are crossed.

### Q: What can the company do with its earned miles?

Exactly four things. First, **transfer miles to any employee's personal SkyMiles account** — immediate, unidirectional, irreversible. This is the most common use. Second, **redeem as Delta eCredits** at approximately 1.5–2 cents per mile, processed in under 4 hours, applicable to future tickets (limit: one eCredit per ticket; since June 2025, also redeemable via GDS and travel agencies). Third, **purchase Sky Club memberships** for individual employees (annual access). Fourth, **grant Silver Medallion certificates** — 12 months of Silver status for a specific employee, processed in one business day.

What the company cannot do: book award flights as an entity, accumulate miles indefinitely without account activity, or apply miles to Basic Economy tickets.

### Q: How does the hub penalty on earning work?

Flights routed through Delta's major hubs — Atlanta (ATL), Detroit (DTW), Minneapolis (MSP), and Salt Lake City (SLC) — earn miles at approximately half the rate of non-hub routes. This is a real cost for companies primarily based in hub cities. The website communicates this transparently with an interactive earning rates table showing route type × cabin class.

### Q: Does the SkyMiles for Business program conflict with my Amex card miles?

No. They are completely parallel systems. Card miles go to the primary cardholder's personal SkyMiles account. Program miles go to the company's SkyMiles for Business account. They don't interact, don't reduce each other, and don't stack into the same pool. Both can run simultaneously on the same flights.

## C. Delta Amex cards — ownership and mechanics

**Pages:** `/cards/compare/`, `/cards/[slug]/`, `/resources/faq/`

### Q: Who owns the Delta Amex business card — the company or a person?

An individual person — specifically the business owner, founder, or an authorized officer. They apply in their own name with personal credit (SSN) plus the company's EIN. American Express underwrites against the individual, not the business entity.

### Q: Where do miles from employee cards go?

All miles from all employee card spending flow into the **primary cardholder's personal SkyMiles account** — not into the company's SkyMiles for Business account, and not into the employee's own SkyMiles account. The primary cardholder accumulates all card miles centrally.

### Q: What do employees get from having an employee card?

Employees get the card's travel benefits — free first checked bag, Zone 5 priority boarding, TakeOff 15 discounts on award redemptions (depending on card tier). But the miles are not theirs. Miles always go to whoever holds the primary account.

### Q: Can I add employee cards, and is there a cost?

Yes — up to 99 employee cards at no additional annual fee. The primary cardholder controls who gets cards and can revoke them.

### Q: How do card miles and SkyMiles for Business miles interact?

They don't. The two systems are parallel and independent. The card earns personal miles for the primary cardholder. The SkyMiles for Business program earns company miles from flights. Neither feeds the other, neither reduces the other. Understanding this separation is essential for companies evaluating the total value of both systems together.

## D. Individual Business Traveler program

**Pages:** `/programs/individual/`, `/resources/faq/`

### Q: What are the actual benefits of the Individual Business Traveler program?

Partner perks for your daily travel life: Hertz Five Star rental status, CLEAR Plus at a discounted rate, Industrious coworking access at $99/month, and LinkedIn Premium discount. These are lifestyle benefits — none of them happen on the plane or at the airport gate.

### Q: Does it help me earn miles faster or get upgrades?

No. Delta Business Traveler does not accelerate Medallion qualification, does not give upgrade priority, does not include Corporate Priority benefits, and does not affect your SkyMiles earning rate. For flight benefits, you need SkyMiles for Business (company enrollment) or Medallion status (personal qualification).

### Q: Why would I join then?

Two reasons. First, if you genuinely use the partner services (Hertz, CLEAR, Industrious, LinkedIn), the bundled discounts have real dollar value. Second, it's the entry point to the Delta business ecosystem — when your company is ready for SkyMiles for Business or a corporate agreement, you're already in the system.

### Q: Is there anything like this at United or American?

No. Delta is the only major US airline that offers a dedicated program for individual business travelers not enrolled through their employer. United and American have no equivalent.

## E. Corporate Priority and enterprise

**Pages:** `/programs/mid-market/`, `/programs/enterprise/`, `/benefits/corporate-priority/`, `/resources/faq/`

### Q: What exactly is Corporate Priority?

Six specific, verifiable benefits that activate automatically on every booking made through your company's Corporate Ticket Designator: preferred seat selection from the moment of ticketing, priority boarding, priority position in the upgrade queue (within the same Medallion tier), standby priority, rebooking priority during disruptions, and overbooking protection. These extend to Air France, KLM, Aeromexico, Virgin Atlantic, and LATAM.

### Q: What's the difference between Mid-Market (MSA) and Enterprise (CSA)?

Not the type of benefit — the depth. Mid-Market gets negotiated fares off published rates plus a dedicated account manager, for companies spending $50K–$300K. Enterprise adds full global Corporate Priority coverage, the unused ticket transfer program (recover credits from departed employees), automatic reporting to travel managers' inboxes, and 24/7 Corporate Solutions phone support.

### Q: How does Delta compare to AA Business Select for large companies?

AA Business Select launched in July 2025 with a $250,000 annual spend threshold — lower than Delta's Elite tier at $300,000. Business Select offers up to 4% fare savings, Group 5 boarding, and immediate access to company-earned miles. However, Delta's Corporate Priority includes six specific priority benefits across five partner airlines that AA cannot match at any tier. The choice depends on whether fare savings or systematic priority treatment is more valuable to your operation. See `/programs/compare/` for the full comparison.

## F. Medallion status and qualification

**Pages:** `/medallion/`, `/medallion/[tier]/`, `/resources/faq/`

### Q: How does Delta Medallion compare to United Premier status?

Since 2024, Delta uses MQD-only qualification — one number: how much you spend. United uses either MQD or MQD + flight segments. At the top tier, both require $28,000 in spending. But United also offers a path at $22,000 + 60 eligible flights. For business travelers who spend heavily but don't fly 60 segments per year, Delta is more achievable. For those who fly very frequently on cheaper fares, United's segment-based path might work. See the detailed comparison at `/medallion/`.

### Q: Does Delta Amex card spending count toward Medallion?

Yes, through two mechanisms. **MQD Headstart** gives you a fixed annual credit toward Medallion qualification just for holding the card (up to $2,500 per card, stackable with a personal + business combo for $5,000). **MQD Boost** earns additional MQDs based on how much you spend on the card throughout the year. With dual Reserve cards, the $5,000 Headstart alone qualifies you for Silver Medallion without flying a single mile.

## G. Competitive and migration questions

**Pages:** `/programs/compare/`, `/resources/faq/`

### Q: What happened to United PerksPlus?

As of February 2024, United offers PerksPlus only to companies booking through a travel agency — no new direct enrollments. Existing accounts can use their points through at least June 30, 2026. If you were a PerksPlus customer, Delta SkyMiles for Business offers free enrollment, self-serve setup, and starts earning from $5,000 annual spend.

### Q: Is Delta always the best choice for business travel?

Not for every route profile. Delta is strongest for US domestic travel (especially from hub cities), transatlantic (Air France/KLM joint venture), and Latin America (LATAM + Aeromexico). For companies with heavy Asia-Pacific travel, United (Star Alliance with ANA and Singapore Airlines) or American (oneworld with JAL, Qantas, and Cathay Pacific) may offer better coverage. The right answer depends on where your team actually flies.

### Q: Which airline has the best business credit card?

Depends on priorities. Delta Reserve ($650) offers Sky Club access with 2 guests and a Delta One companion certificate. United Club Infinite ($525) is cheaper but the companion cert is economy only. AA Executive ($595) offers Admirals Club access but no companion certificate. For Medallion/status acceleration, Delta's MQD Headstart system (up to $5K/year with dual cards) has no equivalent at United or AA since United removed their MQD waiver in 2024.

## H. Enrollment and getting started

**Pages:** `/enroll/small-business/`, `/enroll/individual/`, `/enroll/enterprise/`, `/resources/faq/`

### Q: How long does enrollment take?

Individual Business Traveler: instant, online. SkyMiles for Business (SMB): under 3 minutes, self-serve online. Mid-Market and Enterprise: requires a conversation with Delta Corporate Sales — expect initial contact within 4 business hours after submitting the form.

### Q: What do I need to enroll in SkyMiles for Business?

Company legal name, EIN/Tax ID, industry, and an admin contact. You can add travelers immediately by email or CSV upload, or skip and add them later. Each traveler must link their own personal SkyMiles ID.

### Q: What happens after I enroll?

You receive a Business ID (format: DELTA-XXXXXXX) and an activation email. Your company starts at the Member tier with portal access. Once you reach 5 registered travelers and $5,000 in eligible spend, you automatically upgrade to Plus and begin earning miles. The dashboard is available immediately for tracking.

## I. Double-earn automation and account linking

**Pages:** `/skymiles/double-earn/`, `/programs/small-business/`, `/skymiles/how-it-works/`, `/resources/faq/`

### Q: Is the double-earn automatic if the company has an account and the employee has a SkyMiles account?

Almost — but it requires one setup step. The double-earn is not fully automatic just because both accounts exist. Here's what needs to happen: the company must be enrolled in SkyMiles for Business (free), and the employee must link their personal SkyMiles ID to the company's account through the management portal. Once that link is established, the Business ID auto-applies to every eligible booking the employee makes. From that point forward, yes — every flight automatically earns in both accounts without any manual action per trip. The employee doesn't need to enter anything at checkout. The company doesn't need to approve each flight. The system handles it. But that initial link between the employee's SkyMiles account and the company's Business ID is the one manual step that activates the whole mechanic.

### Q: What if the employee already has Medallion status — does the company benefit from that?

The employee's Medallion status is personal and doesn't transfer to the company account. However, there's an indirect benefit: Medallion members earn personal miles at a higher multiplier (40% bonus for Silver up to 120% for Diamond), which makes them more valuable travelers overall — they get better seats, upgrades, and priority treatment on flights they're taking for the company. The company's earning rate from SkyMiles for Business is based on fare and route, not the employee's personal Medallion tier. So the company earns the same miles whether the traveler is Silver or Diamond. But the employee's flight experience improves significantly with higher status, which benefits the company through traveler satisfaction and productivity.

## J. Medallion status — what it is, how it relates to business travel

**Pages:** `/medallion/`, `/medallion/overview/`, `/programs/small-business/`, `/resources/faq/`

### Q: What is Delta Medallion status and how is it different from SkyMiles?

They are two separate things that people constantly confuse. **SkyMiles** is your mile balance — a spendable currency you earn from flying, card spend, and partners. You can use SkyMiles to book award flights, upgrade, buy lounge access, etc. The balance goes up and down as you earn and spend. **Medallion status** is your elite tier — Silver, Gold, Platinum, or Diamond — earned by accumulating Medallion Qualifying Dollars (MQDs) within a calendar year. Medallion determines how Delta treats you: boarding priority, upgrade eligibility, lounge access, bonus mile earning, and seat selection windows. You qualify for Medallion through spending (MQDs from flights + card acceleration), not by accumulating SkyMiles. You can have a million SkyMiles and no Medallion status, or Diamond status and very few SkyMiles. They're tracked separately, earned differently, and used differently.

### Q: How does Medallion status relate to Delta for Business — what does the company get, what does the employee get?

Medallion is entirely personal — it belongs to the individual employee, not the company. The company cannot earn Medallion status, cannot transfer it, and cannot revoke it. Here's how it intersects with business travel:

**What the employee gets from Medallion (personally):** Priority boarding (zone 3–6 depending on tier), complimentary upgrades when available, Sky Club access (Platinum and Diamond), bonus SkyMiles earning (40–120% extra), longer upgrade windows, and Choice Benefits at Platinum and Diamond. All of this follows the employee whether they're flying for business or personal travel.

**What the company gets indirectly:** Happier, more productive travelers. Employees with Medallion status have better flight experiences — better seats, faster boarding, lounge access on layovers, priority rebooking when things go wrong. The company also benefits because Corporate Priority stacks with Medallion: a Gold Medallion employee flying on a corporate ticket outranks a Gold Medallion non-corporate traveler in the upgrade queue. So the company's Corporate Ticket Designator gives their Medallion employees an extra edge.

**What the company can actively do:** Use company miles to grant Silver Medallion certificates to employees who haven't reached Silver on their own. This is the one way the company can directly influence an employee's status.

### Q: Do you have to apply for Medallion status or is it automatic?

Completely automatic. There is no application, no enrollment form, no approval process. Delta tracks your Medallion Qualifying Dollars (MQDs) throughout the calendar year. MQDs come from two sources: eligible flight spend (base fare on Delta-marketed flights) and Amex card acceleration (MQD Headstart + MQD Boost). When your MQDs cross a threshold — $5,000 for Silver, $10,000 for Gold, $15,000 for Platinum, $28,000 for Diamond — your status activates for the rest of the current year plus the entire following year. You don't need to claim it, request it, or notify Delta. The system updates automatically and your benefits activate immediately. The same applies to card MQD Headstart: if you hold a Delta Platinum or Reserve Amex, the $2,500 Headstart credits to your MQD balance at the start of the year automatically.

## K. eCredits — how they work and what they're worth

**Pages:** `/skymiles/redeeming/`, `/programs/small-business/`, `/resources/faq/`

### Q: How does a company convert SkyMiles to eCredits, and what are eCredits used for?

The company admin initiates the conversion through the SkyMiles for Business management portal. You select the number of miles to convert, confirm, and the eCredits are generated within 4 hours. The conversion rate is approximately 1.5 to 2 cents per mile — so 10,000 miles becomes roughly $150–$200 in eCredits.

**What eCredits are:** They are Delta travel credits that work like money toward future Delta flights. You apply an eCredit at checkout when booking a ticket on delta.com, and it reduces the fare by that amount.

**Key rules:**
- Only one eCredit can be applied per ticket — you cannot stack multiple eCredits on a single booking
- Since June 2025, eCredits are also redeemable through GDS systems and travel agencies, which makes them usable in managed corporate travel workflows (Concur, TMC bookings, etc.)
- eCredits cannot be used for Basic Economy tickets
- eCredits have an expiration date (typically 1 year from issuance)
- The conversion is one-way — once miles become eCredits, they cannot be converted back

**Why companies use eCredits:** They're the most straightforward way to turn company miles into direct travel savings. A company earning 100,000 miles per year from flights can convert that to $1,500–$2,000 in eCredits, effectively getting free flights for future business travel.
