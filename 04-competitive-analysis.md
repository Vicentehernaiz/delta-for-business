# 04 — Competitive Analysis: Delta vs United vs American
## Delta for Business Web Redesign

> **Purpose:** Inform web messaging, comparison page content, and SEO strategy. This document identifies exploitable gaps, honest weaknesses, and competitive positioning for each segment.

---

## 1. Strategic context — why this matters for the web

The user journey includes an **Evaluation stage** where the user compares Delta against competitors before deciding. If the website doesn't answer that comparison directly, the user goes to Google — and may not come back.

Three levels of impact:

1. **Web design** — A `/programs/compare/` page or homepage section answering "Why Delta vs United for Business or AA Corporate" is one of the strongest conversion levers available.
2. **SEO** — Terms like "delta vs united corporate travel", "best airline for business travel program", "delta for business vs american airlines corporate" have real search volume and low competition in quality content. A well-built comparison page can rank for these queries and capture users at the exact moment of evaluation.
3. **Pain points and messaging** — Knowing exactly what United and American do better (or worse) sharpens copy per segment. If United has a better calculator, that validates building ours. If AA has worse partner coverage, that's a differentiator to highlight.

## 3. SME programs — head-to-head

### Delta: SkyMiles for Business

- **Three tiers:** Member (free, no earning) → Plus (5 travelers + $5K → up to 10 miles/$) → Elite ($300K + 15% bonus)
- **Double-earn:** Company earns on fare, employee earns personal miles + MQDs simultaneously — no reduction to either
- **Redemption:** eCredits (~1.5–2¢/mile), transfer to employees, Sky Club memberships, Silver Medallion certificates
- **Minimum to start earning:** $5,000 annual eligible flight revenue + 5 registered travelers
- **Self-serve enrollment** with instant dashboard access

### United: PerksPlus (being phased out)

- **Status:** As of Feb 2024, United offers PerksPlus only to companies booking via a travel agency. All existing accounts can use points through at least June 30, 2026.
- **Earning:** Points on eligible flights, but structure is simpler and less generous than Delta's tiered rates
- **Key weakness:** Program is functionally dead for new direct enrollments. No self-serve path.
- **PassPlus:** Alternative savings program, but not a loyalty earn system

### American Airlines: AAdvantage Business

- **Earning:** Flat 1 extra AAdvantage mile per dollar spent on American flights — incremental 1.5% return
- **Simplicity advantage:** One rate, easy to communicate, no tier complexity
- **Key weakness:** Cannot match Delta's 10 miles/$ on premium non-hub routes. For companies with frequent premium travel, Delta wins clearly.
- **For companies with cheap domestic flights:** AA's simplicity may be easier to sell internally

### Delta's SME advantage

Delta's $5K minimum vs United's $50K Blueprint minimum is the clearest competitive gap. United is abandoning the SME space (PerksPlus dying), and AA's flat earning rate cannot compete on premium routes. **Delta owns the SME corridor** — the web should make this unavoidable.

This is uncontested but the value is limited — partner perks, not flight benefits. Position it as a **funnel entry point**, not a standalone product of equal weight.

## 5. Corporate programs (Mid-Market + Enterprise)

### Delta: Corporate Priority (MSA/CSA)

- **Corporate Priority suite:** Preferred seating, priority boarding, upgrade queue preference, standby priority, rebooking priority, overbooking protection
- **Extends to partners:** AF, KLM, Aeromexico, Virgin Atlantic, LATAM
- **MSA (Mid-Market):** Negotiated fares + account manager, $50K–$300K spend
- **CSA (Enterprise):** Full global coverage, unused ticket transfer, 24/7 Corporate Solutions, $300K+ spend
- **Elite tier bonus:** +15% on all company miles at $300K

### United: Blueprint

- **Minimum:** $50,000 annual air spend — excludes smaller mid-market companies
- **Discount structure:** Negotiated fares off published rates
- **Corporate travel benefits:** Similar priority services but less granular than Delta's Corporate Priority breakdown
- **Key weakness:** The $50K floor locks out the $5K–$50K segment entirely

### American Airlines: Business Extra + Business Select

- **Business Extra:** Earn 1 point per $5 spent on AA flights. Points redeemable for upgrades, Admirals Club passes, AAdvantage miles, or flights.
- **Business Select (launched July 2025):** New premium tier for companies spending $250K+/year
  - Up to 4% savings on eligible fares
  - Preferred Group 5 boarding for all travelers
  - Immediate access to company-earned miles
- **Key threat:** Business Select's $250K threshold undercuts Delta's Elite tier at $300K. Companies spending $250–300K get the premium treatment from AA but not from Delta.

### Competitive gaps to address

- **AA Business Select at $250K** — Delta's Elite threshold is $300K. The web should either highlight benefits that justify the gap or acknowledge it honestly while emphasizing other advantages (partner network, Corporate Priority depth).
- **United's $50K floor** — Delta's messaging for the $5K–$50K segment should explicitly note: "Most airlines require $50K minimum. Delta starts at $5K."

## 6. Elite status comparison

| | **Delta Medallion** | **United Premier** | **AA AAdvantage Elite** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **System** | MQD-only (since 2024) | MQD or MQD + segments | Loyalty Points |
| **Silver / equivalent** | $5,000 MQD | $6,000 or $4,800 + 20 flights | 40,000 LP |
| **Gold / equivalent** | $10,000 MQD | $12,000 or $9,600 + 40 flights | 75,000 LP |
| **Platinum / equivalent** | $15,000 MQD | $18,000 or $14,400 + 50 flights | 125,000 LP |
| **Diamond / 1K equivalent** | $28,000 MQD | $28,000 or $22,000 + 60 flights | 200,000 LP |

### Key insight: United raised requirements ~25% in 2025

United's top-tier Premier 1K now requires $28,000 in spending OR $22,000 + 60 eligible flights. This matches Delta Diamond on spend — but United also demands 60 segments for the cheaper path. **For business travelers who don't fly 60 segments per year, Delta is more achievable.** The web should surface this comparison directly.

### Card acceleration advantage

Delta's Amex card MQD Headstart system (up to $5,000/year with dual cards) has no direct equivalent at this level in United or AA's ecosystem. This is a defensible advantage for travelers who spend heavily on cards but don't fly 60+ times a year.

## 7. Credit cards comparison

| | **Delta Amex** | **United Chase** | **AA Citi / Barclays** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Top personal card** | Reserve ($650) | Club Infinite ($525) | Executive ($595) |
| **Lounge access** | Sky Club (cardholder + 2 guests) | United Club | Admirals Club + guests |
| **Companion cert** | Delta One / First Class | Economy only | None (AA Executive) |
| **MQD acceleration** | Up to $5K Headstart + Boost | MQD waiver gone since 2024 | LP earning from spend |
| **Business cards** | 3 variants (Gold, Platinum, Reserve) | 2 variants | 2 variants |

### Card ownership clarification (critical for web)

Delta Amex business cards are issued to an **individual** (owner/officer), not to the company as a legal entity. The primary cardholder applies with personal credit + business EIN. Up to 99 employee cards can be added at no extra cost, but **all miles from employee card spending flow to the primary cardholder's personal SkyMiles account** — not to the company's SkyMiles for Business account, and not to the employee's own account.

**The card system and SkyMiles for Business are parallel and independent.** The card earns personal miles for the owner. The program earns company miles from flights. Neither feeds the other. This is one of the most misunderstood aspects of the ecosystem and must be clearly communicated on the cards page.

| **Transatlantic** | Strong (AF/KLM JV) | Strong (Lufthansa JV) | Strong (BA/Iberia JV) |

### Route-based messaging

For companies primarily flying US domestic and transatlantic: Delta or United. For companies with heavy Asia-Pacific travel: United or American may be stronger. For Latin America: Delta's LATAM + Aeromexico JV is competitive. The web should acknowledge geographic fit honestly in the comparison page.

## 10. Delta's defensible advantages (for web messaging)

1. **Only airline with an individual traveler program** — uncontested, zero competition
2. **$5K minimum vs $50K (United Blueprint)** — Delta owns the SME corridor
3. **Double-earn mechanic** — company + employee earn simultaneously, no trade-off. Neither United nor AA offers this clearly.
4. **MQD-only status qualification** — simpler than United's dual-path (spend OR spend + segments). For business travelers who don't fly 60 segments, Delta is more reachable.
5. **Card MQD Headstart** — up to $5K/year toward status from card spend alone. No equivalent at this level from competitors.
6. **Corporate Priority extends to SkyTeam partners** — AF, KLM, LATAM, VA, Aeromexico. Breadth of partner priority coverage.
7. **United PerksPlus is dying** — displaced SMEs are looking for a new home. Acquisition opportunity is real and time-limited.

---

## 11. Delta's honest gaps (acknowledge or address)

1. **AA Business Select threshold ($250K) undercuts Delta Elite ($300K)** — companies at $250–300K get premium treatment from AA but not Delta. The web should emphasize other benefits that compensate, or the messaging should acknowledge the gap while highlighting Corporate Priority's depth.
2. **Asia-Pacific network** — United (Star Alliance) and American (oneworld) have stronger JV coverage in Asia. Delta's Korean Air JV helps but doesn't fully close the gap.
3. **Delta Business Traveler has no flight benefit** — partner perks only. Don't oversell it as equal to SkyMiles for Business or Corporate Priority.
4. **Hub penalty on earning rates** — flights through ATL, DTW, MSP, SLC earn at half the rate. This is a real disadvantage for companies based in hub cities. Must be communicated transparently.
5. **eCredit limitation** — only one eCredit per ticket. For companies wanting to stack credits, this is a friction point.

## 12. Competitive messaging framework (ready for web copy)

### For the comparison page (`/programs/compare/`)

**Against United (SME):**
> "Most airlines require $50,000 minimum annual spend. Delta SkyMiles for Business starts at $5,000. If your company flies Delta even a few times a year, you're already eligible."

**Against United (PerksPlus migration):**
> "Were you a United PerksPlus customer? That program is no longer accepting new enrollments. Delta SkyMiles for Business is free to join and starts earning from day one."

**Against AA (earning rates):**
> "AAdvantage Business earns a flat 1 mile per dollar on every flight. Delta SkyMiles for Business earns up to 10 miles per dollar on premium non-hub routes. For companies with frequent premium travel, the difference compounds fast."

**Against AA Business Select (enterprise):**
> "AA Business Select requires $250,000 annual spend. Delta Corporate Priority activates with your first managed agreement and includes six specific priority benefits — from seating selection to disruption rebooking — across Delta and five partner airlines."

**Against United (status):**
> "United Premier 1K requires $28,000 in spending or $22,000 plus 60 flights. Delta Diamond requires $28,000 — period. No flight count. If your team spends but doesn't fly 60 segments, Delta gets you to top-tier status faster."

### For segment pages (subtle competitive context)

- **Individual page:** "Delta is the only major US airline with a dedicated program for individual business travelers. No employer enrollment required."
- **SMB page:** "Free enrollment. $5,000 minimum to start earning — the lowest threshold of any major US airline business program."
- **Mid-Market page:** "Corporate Priority includes six specific priority benefits that extend across SkyTeam partner airlines — coverage that competitor programs cannot match at this spend level."
- **Enterprise page:** "Unused ticket transfer, 24/7 Corporate Solutions support, and global Corporate Priority on five partner airlines. Built for companies where travel disruption has real financial impact."
